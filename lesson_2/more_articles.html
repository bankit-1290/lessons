See Sri Lanka’s misty tea country—in pictures

Sri Lanka’s Up Country is a landscape of forested peaks and rolling hills, their slopes striped with tea plantations and dotted with picket-fenced railway stations and colonial-era resorts.

Story and photographs by Mark Parren Taylor
Published February 7, 2026

Interesting facts

  1. The tea region Up Country — the heart of the highlands
    • Geographic location
      • located in the central part of the island
      • surrounded by mountains and hills
      • has a cooler climate
    • Main settlements
      • Nuwara Eliya — “Little England”
      • Ella — a tourist center
      • small mountain villages nearby
  2. Tea plantations create a unique landscape
    • Appearance of the plantations
      • hills covered with neat rows of tea bushes
      • create a “green waves” effect
      • carefully maintained by hand
    • Historical origin
      • developed during the British Empire
      • replaced coffee plantations
      • became the economic foundation of the region
  3. Railways are an important part of the region
    • Route features
      • pass through mountains and tunnels
      • cross bridges and valleys
      • offer panoramic views
    • Popular routes
      • Kandy–Ella railway
      • popular among tourists
      • slow travel for enjoying the scenery
  4. The region’s nature is known for mist and forests
    • Climate characteristics
      • frequent mist
      • high humidity
      • cool nights
    • Natural attractions
      • Horton Plains National Park
      • plateaus and cliffs
      • rare animal species
  5. Colonial heritage is still visible today
    • Architecture
      • English-style houses
      • old hotels and villas
      • well-kept gardens
    • Cultural influence
      • tea-drinking traditions
      • English place names
      • a historic atmosphere
